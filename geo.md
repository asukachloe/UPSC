# 1. BASIC GEOGRAPHY TERMS

## Geography
The study of the Earth, its physical features, atmosphere, oceans, climate, resources, people, settlements, and human-environment interactions.

## Physical Geography
Branch of geography studying natural features such as landforms, climate, oceans, soils, vegetation, and ecosystems.

## Human Geography
Branch of geography studying population, settlements, migration, culture, economy, transport, and human activities.

## Economic Geography
Study of location and distribution of economic activities such as agriculture, mining, industry, trade, and services.

## Political Geography
Study of political boundaries, states, geopolitics, and territorial organization.

## Regional Geography
Study of a specific region by combining physical, human, and economic aspects.

## Environmental Geography
Study of interaction between humans and environment.

## Geomorphology
Study of landforms and processes shaping the Earth’s surface.

## Climatology
Study of climate and atmospheric processes.

## Oceanography
Study of oceans, seas, currents, waves, tides, marine resources, and ocean processes.

## Biogeography
Study of distribution of plants, animals, and ecosystems.

## Cartography
Science and art of map-making.

## Remote Sensing
Collection of information about Earth’s surface using satellites or aircraft.

## GIS
Geographic Information System; computer-based system for storing, analysing, and displaying spatial data.

## GPS
Global Positioning System used for location and navigation.

## Spatial Distribution
Arrangement of features across space.

## Location
Position of a place on Earth.

## Absolute Location
Exact location using latitude and longitude.

## Relative Location
Location described in relation to other places.

## Site
Actual physical location of a settlement or feature.

## Situation
Location of a place in relation to surrounding areas and routes.

## Region
Area with common physical, cultural, political, or economic characteristics.

## Formal Region
Region with uniform characteristics, such as climate region or language region.

## Functional Region
Region organized around a centre, such as a city-region.

## Perceptual Region
Region based on people’s perception or identity.

## Landscape
Visible features of an area, including landforms, vegetation, water, and human structures.

## Scale
Ratio between distance on map and actual distance on ground.

## Large-Scale Map
Map showing small area with great detail.

## Small-Scale Map
Map showing large area with less detail.

## Topographic Map
Map showing natural and human-made features with relief.

## Thematic Map
Map showing one theme, such as rainfall, population, or soil.

## Contour
Line joining points of equal elevation.

## Isoline
Line joining points of equal value.

## Isotherm
Line joining places of equal temperature.

## Isohyet
Line joining places of equal rainfall.

## Isobar
Line joining places of equal atmospheric pressure.

## Isohaline
Line joining places of equal salinity.

## Bathymetry
Measurement and mapping of ocean depth.

## Relief
Difference in elevation and shape of land surface.

## Elevation
Height above mean sea level.

## Altitude
Height of an object or place above sea level.

## Latitude
Angular distance north or south of the Equator.

## Longitude
Angular distance east or west of the Prime Meridian.

## Equator
0° latitude dividing Earth into Northern and Southern Hemispheres.

## Prime Meridian
0° longitude passing through Greenwich.

## Hemisphere
Half of Earth divided by Equator or Prime Meridian.

## Tropic of Cancer
23.5° N latitude.

## Tropic of Capricorn
23.5° S latitude.

## Arctic Circle
66.5° N latitude.

## Antarctic Circle
66.5° S latitude.

## International Date Line
Imaginary line near 180° longitude where calendar date changes.

## Time Zone
Region following the same standard time.

## Indian Standard Time
Time based on 82.5° E longitude, 5 hours 30 minutes ahead of UTC.

## Great Circle
Largest possible circle on Earth’s surface, useful for shortest air routes.

## Small Circle
Any circle on Earth smaller than a great circle.

# 2. EARTH AS A PLANET

## Earth
Third planet from the Sun and the only known planet supporting life.

## Solar System
The Sun, planets, moons, asteroids, comets, meteoroids, and other objects bound by gravity.

## Planet
Large celestial body orbiting a star.

## Dwarf Planet
Celestial body orbiting the Sun, nearly round, but not clearing its orbital path.

## Satellite
Object orbiting a planet.

## Moon
Natural satellite of Earth.

## Rotation
Earth’s spinning on its axis, causing day and night.

## Revolution
Earth’s movement around the Sun, causing seasons.

## Axis
Imaginary line passing through Earth’s poles.

## Axial Tilt
Earth’s axis is tilted about 23.5°, causing seasonal variation.

## Solstice
Time when Sun is overhead at Tropic of Cancer or Capricorn, producing longest or shortest day.

## Summer Solstice
Around June 21, when Northern Hemisphere has longest day.

## Winter Solstice
Around December 22, when Northern Hemisphere has shortest day.

## Equinox
Time when day and night are nearly equal.

## Vernal Equinox
Around March 21.

## Autumnal Equinox
Around September 23.

## Perihelion
Point when Earth is closest to the Sun.

## Aphelion
Point when Earth is farthest from the Sun.

## Circle of Illumination
Line separating day and night on Earth.

## Coriolis Force
Apparent force caused by Earth’s rotation, deflecting winds and currents right in Northern Hemisphere and left in Southern Hemisphere.

## Centrifugal Force
Outward force due to rotation.

## Gravity
Force attracting objects toward Earth’s centre.

## Geoid
Earth’s true shape, slightly flattened at poles and bulging at equator.

## Oblate Spheroid
Shape of Earth with equatorial bulge and polar flattening.

# 3. EARTH’S INTERIOR

## Earth’s Interior
Internal structure of Earth consisting of crust, mantle, and core.

## Crust
Outermost solid layer of Earth.

## Continental Crust
Thicker, lighter crust forming continents; mainly granitic.

## Oceanic Crust
Thinner, denser crust forming ocean floors; mainly basaltic.

## Mantle
Layer below crust, rich in silicate minerals.

## Upper Mantle
Upper part of mantle, includes asthenosphere.

## Lower Mantle
Deeper, more rigid mantle layer.

## Core
Innermost layer of Earth, mainly iron and nickel.

## Outer Core
Liquid metallic layer responsible for Earth’s magnetic field.

## Inner Core
Solid metallic central part of Earth.

## Lithosphere
Rigid outer layer including crust and uppermost mantle.

## Asthenosphere
Semi-molten layer below lithosphere on which plates move.

## Mesosphere
Lower mantle region below asthenosphere.

## Barysphere
Older term for Earth’s core.

## SIAL
Continental crust rich in silica and aluminium.

## SIMA
Oceanic crust rich in silica and magnesium.

## NIFE
Core rich in nickel and iron.

## Moho Discontinuity
Boundary between crust and mantle.

## Gutenberg Discontinuity
Boundary between mantle and outer core.

## Lehmann Discontinuity
Boundary between outer core and inner core.

## Seismic Waves
Waves produced by earthquakes, used to study Earth’s interior.

## P-Waves
Primary seismic waves that travel through solids, liquids, and gases.

## S-Waves
Secondary seismic waves that travel only through solids.

## Surface Waves
Seismic waves travelling along Earth’s surface, often most destructive.

## Shadow Zone
Area where seismic waves are not recorded due to refraction and Earth’s internal structure.

# 4. PLATE TECTONICS

## Plate Tectonics
Theory explaining movement of lithospheric plates over asthenosphere.

## Lithospheric Plate
Rigid section of Earth’s lithosphere.

## Continental Plate
Plate carrying continental crust.

## Oceanic Plate
Plate carrying oceanic crust.

## Plate Boundary
Zone where two plates meet.

## Divergent Boundary
Boundary where plates move apart.

## Convergent Boundary
Boundary where plates move toward each other.

## Transform Boundary
Boundary where plates slide past each other.

## Constructive Boundary
Divergent boundary where new crust forms.

## Destructive Boundary
Convergent boundary where crust is destroyed by subduction.

## Conservative Boundary
Transform boundary where crust is neither created nor destroyed.

## Seafloor Spreading
Formation of new oceanic crust at mid-ocean ridges.

## Mid-Ocean Ridge
Underwater mountain chain formed at divergent boundaries.

## Rift Valley
Valley formed by divergence or crustal stretching.

## Subduction
Process where one plate sinks beneath another.

## Trench
Deep oceanic depression formed at subduction zones.

## Island Arc
Chain of volcanic islands formed above subduction zones.

## Volcanic Arc
Chain of volcanoes formed above subduction zones.

## Orogeny
Mountain-building process.

## Fold Mountain
Mountain formed by compression and folding of rocks.

## Block Mountain
Mountain formed by faulting and uplift.

## Residual Mountain
Mountain remaining after erosion of surrounding areas.

## Ocean-Continent Convergence
Oceanic plate subducts beneath continental plate.

## Ocean-Ocean Convergence
One oceanic plate subducts beneath another, forming island arcs.

## Continent-Continent Convergence
Two continental plates collide, forming high fold mountains.

## Himalayan Orogeny
Formation of Himalayas due to collision of Indian Plate with Eurasian Plate.

## Indian Plate
Tectonic plate carrying India and parts of Indian Ocean.

## Eurasian Plate
Plate with which Indian Plate collided to form Himalayas.

## Ring of Fire
Pacific Ocean belt of volcanoes and earthquakes.

## Hotspot
Volcanic region caused by mantle plume away from plate boundaries.

## Mantle Plume
Upwelling of hot mantle material.

## Deccan Trap
Large basaltic volcanic province in India formed by fissure eruptions.

# 5. ROCKS AND MINERALS

## Rock
Natural solid mass of minerals.

## Mineral
Naturally occurring inorganic substance with definite chemical composition.

## Igneous Rock
Rock formed from cooling and solidification of magma or lava.

## Sedimentary Rock
Rock formed by deposition, compaction, and cementation of sediments.

## Metamorphic Rock
Rock changed by heat, pressure, or chemical processes.

## Intrusive Igneous Rock
Rock formed when magma cools inside Earth.

## Extrusive Igneous Rock
Rock formed when lava cools on Earth’s surface.

## Granite
Coarse-grained intrusive igneous rock.

## Basalt
Fine-grained extrusive igneous rock.

## Gabbro
Coarse-grained intrusive igneous rock similar to basalt.

## Obsidian
Volcanic glass.

## Pumice
Light porous volcanic rock.

## Sediment
Particles of rock, mineral, or organic material.

## Sandstone
Sedimentary rock made of sand-sized particles.

## Shale
Sedimentary rock made of clay-sized particles.

## Limestone
Sedimentary rock mainly composed of calcium carbonate.

## Conglomerate
Sedimentary rock made of rounded pebbles.

## Breccia
Sedimentary rock made of angular fragments.

## Coal
Organic sedimentary rock formed from plant remains.

## Marble
Metamorphosed limestone.

## Slate
Metamorphosed shale.

## Schist
Metamorphic rock with visible mineral grains and foliation.

## Gneiss
High-grade metamorphic rock with banding.

## Quartzite
Metamorphosed sandstone.

## Rock Cycle
Continuous transformation of rocks among igneous, sedimentary, and metamorphic forms.

## Magma
Molten rock below Earth’s surface.

## Lava
Molten rock reaching Earth’s surface.

## Weathering
Breakdown of rocks in place.

## Erosion
Removal and transport of weathered material.

## Deposition
Laying down of transported material.

## Lithification
Conversion of sediments into rock.

## Metamorphism
Change of rock due to heat and pressure.

# 6. GEOMORPHOLOGY: LANDFORM PROCESSES

## Geomorphic Process
Natural process shaping landforms.

## Endogenic Process
Internal Earth process such as tectonics, earthquakes, and volcanism.

## Exogenic Process
External process such as weathering, erosion, and deposition.

## Diastrophism
Deformation of Earth’s crust due to internal forces.

## Folding
Bending of rock layers due to compression.

## Faulting
Breaking and displacement of rocks.

## Fault
Fracture along which movement occurs.

## Normal Fault
Fault caused by tension where hanging wall moves down.

## Reverse Fault
Fault caused by compression where hanging wall moves up.

## Thrust Fault
Low-angle reverse fault.

## Strike-Slip Fault
Fault where rocks move horizontally.

## Anticline
Upfold in rock layers.

## Syncline
Downfold in rock layers.

## Joint
Fracture in rock without significant movement.

## Weathering
Breakdown of rocks at or near Earth’s surface.

## Mechanical Weathering
Physical breakdown of rocks without chemical change.

## Chemical Weathering
Decomposition of rocks through chemical reactions.

## Biological Weathering
Breakdown of rocks by plants, animals, and organisms.

## Exfoliation
Peeling of rock layers due to temperature change or pressure release.

## Frost Action
Breaking of rocks by freeze-thaw cycles.

## Hydration
Chemical weathering by absorption of water.

## Hydrolysis
Chemical reaction of minerals with water.

## Oxidation
Reaction of minerals with oxygen.

## Carbonation
Reaction of minerals with carbonic acid.

## Mass Wasting
Downslope movement of rock and soil under gravity.

## Landslide
Rapid downslope movement of rock, soil, and debris.

## Soil Creep
Very slow downslope movement of soil.

## Mudflow
Flow of water-saturated fine material.

## Debris Flow
Rapid flow of mixed rock, soil, and water.

## Slump
Rotational slide of material along curved surface.

## Denudation
Overall lowering of land surface by weathering, erosion, and mass wasting.

## Erosion
Wearing away and transport of surface material.

## Deposition
Accumulation of eroded material.

## Gradation
Levelling of Earth’s surface by erosion and deposition.

# 7. FLUVIAL LANDFORMS

## Fluvial Process
Process related to rivers and streams.

## River
Natural flowing water channel.

## Stream
Small flowing watercourse.

## Drainage Basin
Area drained by a river and its tributaries.

## Watershed
Highland separating two drainage basins.

## Catchment Area
Area from which a river collects water.

## Tributary
Smaller river joining a larger river.

## Distributary
Branch of a river flowing away from main channel near delta.

## River Source
Origin of a river.

## River Mouth
Place where river enters sea, lake, or another river.

## River Course
Path followed by a river.

## Upper Course
Mountainous part of river with erosion dominant.

## Middle Course
Part where river has both erosion and deposition.

## Lower Course
Plain area where deposition dominates.

## Vertical Erosion
Downward cutting by river.

## Lateral Erosion
Sideways erosion by river.

## Headward Erosion
Upstream extension of river channel.

## River Capture
One river captures headwaters of another.

## Gorge
Deep narrow valley with steep sides.

## Canyon
Large deep valley with steep sides.

## V-Shaped Valley
Valley formed by vertical river erosion.

## Waterfall
Sudden vertical drop in river course.

## Rapids
Fast-flowing turbulent river section.

## Pothole
Circular hole eroded in river bed.

## Meander
Curving bend of a river.

## Ox-Bow Lake
Crescent-shaped lake formed when a meander is cut off.

## Floodplain
Flat land beside river formed by deposition during floods.

## Natural Levee
Raised bank formed by river deposition.

## Point Bar
Deposition on inner side of meander.

## Cut Bank
Eroded outer side of meander.

## Delta
Depositional landform at river mouth.

## Estuary
Tidal mouth of a river where freshwater mixes with seawater.

## Alluvial Fan
Fan-shaped deposit at mountain foot.

## Braided River
River divided into multiple channels by sandbars.

## River Terrace
Step-like landform along river valley.

## Rejuvenation
Renewed river erosion due to uplift or fall in sea level.

## Knick Point
Sharp break in river profile.

## Graded Profile
Smooth concave long profile of mature river.

## Base Level
Lowest level to which a river can erode.

# 8. GLACIAL LANDFORMS

## Glacier
Large mass of moving ice.

## Valley Glacier
Glacier flowing through a mountain valley.

## Continental Glacier
Large ice sheet covering vast land area.

## Ice Sheet
Massive continuous cover of ice.

## Ice Cap
Smaller dome-shaped ice mass.

## Snowline
Lowest level where snow persists year-round.

## Firn
Compacted granular snow becoming glacial ice.

## Glacial Erosion
Erosion by moving ice.

## Plucking
Glacier pulls rock fragments from bedrock.

## Abrasion
Glacier scrapes rock surface with embedded debris.

## U-Shaped Valley
Valley carved by glacier with broad floor and steep sides.

## Cirque
Bowl-shaped hollow at head of glacier.

## Tarn
Small lake in a cirque.

## Arete
Sharp ridge between two glacial valleys.

## Horn
Pyramid-shaped peak formed by erosion from multiple cirques.

## Hanging Valley
Tributary glacial valley left above main valley.

## Fjord
Deep glacial valley flooded by sea.

## Moraine
Deposit of unsorted glacial debris.

## Lateral Moraine
Moraine along sides of glacier.

## Medial Moraine
Moraine in middle of glacier formed by joining lateral moraines.

## Terminal Moraine
Moraine at farthest advance of glacier.

## Ground Moraine
Till spread beneath glacier.

## Till
Unsorted glacial deposit.

## Outwash Plain
Plain formed by meltwater deposits.

## Drumlin
Streamlined hill of glacial till.

## Esker
Long winding ridge of sand and gravel deposited by meltwater.

## Kettle Lake
Lake formed in depression left by melting ice block.

## Aeolian Process
Process related to wind erosion, transport, and deposition.

## Deflation
Removal of loose particles by wind.

## Abrasion
Sand-blasting action of wind.

## Attrition
Rounding of particles by collision.

## Desert Pavement
Surface covered by coarse stones after fine particles are removed.

## Blowout
Depression formed by wind deflation.

## Yardang
Streamlined ridge carved by wind erosion.

## Zeugen
Wind-eroded ridge in desert.

## Ventifact
Rock polished or shaped by wind-blown sand.

## Mushroom Rock
Rock with narrow base and wider top due to wind erosion near ground.

## Sand Dune
Hill of sand deposited by wind.

## Barchan
Crescent-shaped dune with horns pointing downwind.

## Seif Dune
Long narrow dune parallel to wind direction.

## Transverse Dune
Dune perpendicular to wind direction.

## Longitudinal Dune
Long dune parallel to prevailing wind.

## Star Dune
Dune with arms radiating in multiple directions.

## Loess
Fine wind-blown silt deposit.

## Erg
Large sandy desert.

## Reg
Stony desert surface.

## Hamada
Rocky desert surface.

## Oasis
Fertile area in desert where water is available.

## Coast
Zone where land meets sea.

## Shore
Area between low tide and high tide lines.

## Coastline
Boundary between land and sea.

## Wave
Movement of energy through water caused by wind.

## Swash
Water moving up the beach after wave breaks.

## Backwash
Water moving back to sea.

## Longshore Drift
Movement of sediment along coast by angled waves.

## Coastal Erosion
Wearing away of coast by waves and currents.

## Hydraulic Action
Wave pressure breaking rocks.

## Corrasion
Erosion by rock fragments carried by waves.

## Attrition
Rounding of pebbles by collision.

## Solution
Chemical dissolution of coastal rocks.

## Sea Cliff
Steep rock face formed by wave erosion.

## Wave-Cut Platform
Flat surface at base of cliff formed by wave erosion.

## Sea Cave
Cave formed by wave erosion.

## Sea Arch
Arch formed when cave cuts through headland.

## Sea Stack
Isolated pillar of rock left after arch collapse.

## Sea Stump
Eroded remnant of stack.

## Beach
Accumulation of sand, pebbles, or shells along shore.

## Spit
Narrow ridge of sand extending into sea.

## Bar
Ridge of sand blocking bay or river mouth.

## Lagoon
Shallow water body separated from sea by barrier.

## Tombolo
Sand ridge connecting island to mainland.

## Barrier Island
Long narrow island parallel to coast.

## Estuary
Tidal river mouth.

## Delta
River-deposited landform at coast.

## Coral Reef
Limestone structure built by coral polyps.

## Fringing Reef
Coral reef attached to coast.

## Barrier Reef
Reef separated from coast by lagoon.

## Atoll
Ring-shaped coral reef enclosing lagoon.

## Mangrove Coast
Coast dominated by salt-tolerant mangrove vegetation.

## Sinkhole

## Doline
Small closed depression in karst.

## Uvala

## Polje
Large flat-floored karst basin.

## Cave
Underground hollow formed by dissolution.

## Cavern
Large cave chamber.

## Stalactite
Deposit hanging from cave roof.

## Stalagmite
Deposit growing upward from cave floor.

## Pillar
Column formed when stalactite and stalagmite join.

## Underground Stream
Stream flowing through caves.

## Resurgence
Point where underground stream reappears at surface.

## Karst Spring
Spring emerging from limestone terrain.

# 12. VOLCANOES AND EARTHQUAKES

## Volcano
Opening in Earth’s crust through which lava, ash, and gases erupt.

## Magma Chamber
Underground reservoir of magma.

## Vent
Opening through which volcanic material erupts.

## Crater
Bowl-shaped opening at top of volcano.

## Caldera
Large depression formed by collapse after major eruption.

## Lava
Molten rock on Earth’s surface.

## Pyroclastic Material
Fragmented volcanic material like ash, lapilli, and bombs.

## Volcanic Ash
Fine particles erupted by volcano.

## Lapilli
Small volcanic fragments.

## Volcanic Bomb
Large molten rock fragment ejected during eruption.

## Shield Volcano
Broad gentle volcano formed by fluid lava.

## Composite Volcano
Steep volcano made of lava and ash layers.

## Stratovolcano
Another name for composite volcano.

## Cinder Cone
Small volcano made of pyroclastic fragments.

## Fissure Eruption
Lava eruption through long cracks.

## Deccan Traps
Large basaltic plateau formed by fissure eruptions in western and central India.

## Active Volcano
Volcano that erupts or shows signs of activity.

## Dormant Volcano
Volcano currently quiet but may erupt again.

## Extinct Volcano
Volcano unlikely to erupt again.

## Barren Island
India’s only active volcano, located in Andaman Sea.

## Narcondam
Dormant volcanic island in Andaman region.

## Earthquake
Sudden shaking of Earth due to release of energy.

## Focus
Point inside Earth where earthquake starts.

## Hypocentre
Another name for earthquake focus.

## Epicentre
Point on surface directly above focus.

## Fault Line
Fracture zone where earthquakes may occur.

## Seismic Zone
Region classified by earthquake risk.

## Seismograph
Instrument recording earthquake waves.

## Richter Scale
Scale measuring earthquake magnitude.

## Moment Magnitude Scale
Modern scale measuring earthquake energy.

## Mercalli Scale
Scale measuring earthquake intensity based on damage.

## Tsunami
Large sea waves caused by undersea earthquake, volcanic eruption, or landslide.

## Liquefaction
Soil loses strength and behaves like liquid during earthquake.

## Aftershock
Smaller earthquake following main earthquake.

## Foreshock
Smaller earthquake before main earthquake.

# 13. ATMOSPHERE

## Atmosphere
Layer of gases surrounding Earth.

## Weather
Short-term condition of atmosphere.

## Climate
Long-term average weather conditions.

## Meteorology
Study of weather and atmospheric processes.

## Troposphere
Lowest atmospheric layer where weather occurs.

## Stratosphere
Layer above troposphere containing ozone layer.

## Mesosphere
Layer where meteors burn.

## Thermosphere
Layer with very high temperatures and ionosphere.

## Exosphere
Outermost atmospheric layer.

## Tropopause
Boundary between troposphere and stratosphere.

## Ozone Layer
Layer in stratosphere absorbing harmful ultraviolet radiation.

## Ionosphere
Layer containing charged particles, important for radio communication.

## Insolation
Incoming solar radiation.

## Terrestrial Radiation
Heat radiation emitted by Earth.

## Heat Budget
Balance between incoming solar radiation and outgoing terrestrial radiation.

## Albedo
Reflectivity of a surface.

## Greenhouse Effect
Warming caused by gases trapping outgoing heat.

## Greenhouse Gas
Gas such as carbon dioxide, methane, nitrous oxide, and water vapour.

## Temperature
Degree of hotness or coldness.

## Diurnal Range
Difference between daily maximum and minimum temperature.

## Annual Range
Difference between hottest and coldest month temperatures.

## Lapse Rate
Rate at which temperature decreases with height.

## Normal Lapse Rate
Average temperature decrease of about 6.5°C per km in troposphere.

## Inversion of Temperature
Condition where temperature increases with height.

## Urban Heat Island
Urban area warmer than surrounding rural area.

## Heat Wave
Period of abnormally high temperature.

## Cold Wave
Period of abnormally low temperature.

# 14. ATMOSPHERIC PRESSURE AND WINDS

## Atmospheric Pressure
Weight of air above a surface.

## High Pressure
Area where air descends and diverges.

## Low Pressure
Area where air rises and converges.

## Isobar
Line joining places of equal pressure.

## Pressure Gradient
Rate of change of pressure over distance.

## Pressure Gradient Force
Force moving air from high pressure to low pressure.

## Coriolis Force
Force deflecting moving air due to Earth’s rotation.

## Frictional Force
Force slowing wind near Earth’s surface.

## Geostrophic Wind
Wind flowing parallel to isobars due to balance between pressure gradient and Coriolis force.

## Wind
Horizontal movement of air.

## Planetary Winds
Permanent global winds.

## Trade Winds
Winds blowing from subtropical highs to equatorial low.

## Westerlies
Winds blowing from subtropical highs to subpolar lows.

## Polar Easterlies
Cold winds blowing from polar highs to subpolar lows.

## Doldrums
Equatorial low-pressure belt with calm rising air.

## Horse Latitudes
Subtropical high-pressure belts with descending air.

## ITCZ
Inter-Tropical Convergence Zone where trade winds converge.

## Jet Stream
Fast-flowing narrow air current in upper atmosphere.

## Subtropical Westerly Jet
Upper-air westerly jet influencing winter weather in India.

## Tropical Easterly Jet
Upper-air easterly jet associated with Indian summer monsoon.

## Local Wind
Wind limited to a small region.

## Land Breeze
Night wind from land to sea.

## Sea Breeze
Day wind from sea to land.

## Valley Breeze
Day wind blowing upslope.

## Mountain Breeze
Night wind blowing downslope.

## Chinook
Warm dry wind on leeward side of Rocky Mountains.

## Foehn
Warm dry wind on leeward side of Alps.

## Loo
Hot dry summer wind of north-west India.

## Mistral
Cold wind blowing into Mediterranean region.

## Bora
Cold dry wind blowing from mountains to Adriatic coast.

## Sirocco
Hot dusty wind from Sahara toward Mediterranean.

## Harmattan
Dry dusty wind in West Africa.

# 15. HUMIDITY, CLOUDS, AND PRECIPITATION

## Humidity
Amount of water vapour in air.

## Absolute Humidity
Actual amount of water vapour in air.

## Relative Humidity
Percentage of water vapour present compared to maximum possible at that temperature.

## Specific Humidity
Mass of water vapour per unit mass of air.

## Dew Point
Temperature at which air becomes saturated.

## Condensation
Conversion of water vapour into liquid water.

## Evaporation
Conversion of liquid water into vapour.

## Transpiration
Release of water vapour by plants.

## Evapotranspiration

# 47. HIGH-YIELD PHYSICAL GEOGRAPHY COMPARISONS

## Weather vs Climate
Weather is short-term atmospheric condition. Climate is long-term average weather.

## Rotation vs Revolution
Rotation causes day and night. Revolution with axial tilt causes seasons.

## P-Waves vs S-Waves
P-waves pass through solids and liquids. S-waves pass only through solids.

## Continental Crust vs Oceanic Crust
Continental crust is thicker and lighter. Oceanic crust is thinner and denser.

## Convergent vs Divergent Boundary
Convergent plates move together. Divergent plates move apart.

## Weathering vs Erosion
Weathering breaks rocks in place. Erosion removes and transports material.

## Delta vs Estuary
Delta is depositional river mouth. Estuary is tidal river mouth.

## Gorge vs Canyon
Both are deep valleys; canyon is usually larger and often in arid regions.

## Barchan vs Seif Dune
Barchan is crescent-shaped. Seif is long and narrow.

## Stalactite vs Stalagmite
Stalactite hangs from cave roof. Stalagmite grows from cave floor.

## Warm Current vs Cold Current
Warm currents raise coastal temperature. Cold currents lower coastal temperature and often support fisheries.

## Spring Tide vs Neap Tide
Spring tide has highest tidal range. Neap tide has lowest tidal range.

# 48. HIGH-YIELD INDIAN GEOGRAPHY COMPARISONS

## Bhabar vs Terai
Bhabar is porous pebble belt where streams disappear. Terai is marshy belt where streams reappear.

## Bhangar vs Khadar
Bhangar is older alluvium. Khadar is newer, more fertile floodplain alluvium.

## Western Ghats vs Eastern Ghats
Western Ghats are continuous and high. Eastern Ghats are discontinuous and lower.

## Western Coastal Plain vs Eastern Coastal Plain
Western coastal plain is narrow. Eastern coastal plain is wider and has large deltas.

## Himalayan Rivers vs Peninsular Rivers
Himalayan rivers are mostly perennial and form deep valleys. Peninsular rivers are older, mostly seasonal, and have broad valleys.

## Delta vs Estuary in India
East-flowing peninsular rivers often form deltas. West-flowing rivers like Narmada and Tapi form estuaries.

## Southwest Monsoon vs Northeast Monsoon
Southwest monsoon gives most rainfall to India. Northeast monsoon gives major rain to Tamil Nadu.

## Arabian Sea Branch vs Bay of Bengal Branch
Arabian Sea branch gives rain to west coast and northwest India. Bay branch gives rain to northeast and Gangetic plains.

## Alluvial Soil vs Black Soil
Alluvial soil is river-deposited. Black soil is basalt-derived and good for cotton.

## Red Soil vs Laterite Soil
Red soil is iron-rich with less leaching. Laterite soil is heavily leached in high rainfall areas.

# 52. CHRONOLOGICAL STORY TIMELINE OF GEOGRAPHY

## Stage 1: Earth Formation
Earth formed as a planet with layered interior, atmosphere, oceans, and crust.

## Stage 2: Plate Tectonic Evolution
Movement of plates shaped continents, oceans, mountains, volcanoes, and earthquakes.

## Stage 3: Indian Plate Movement
Indian Plate moved northward and collided with Eurasian Plate.

## Stage 4: Himalayan Formation
Himalayas rose due to continent-continent collision and continue to rise.

## Stage 5: Peninsular Stability
Indian Peninsula remained an old stable shield with plateaus, minerals, and ancient rocks.

## Stage 6: Formation of Northern Plains
Rivers from Himalayas deposited alluvium and created the Indo-Gangetic-Brahmaputra plains.

## Stage 7: Monsoon Development
Seasonal heating and pressure systems created monsoon climate over South Asia.

## Stage 8: River and Soil Systems
Rivers, weathering, and climate created India’s alluvial, black, red, laterite, desert, and forest soils.

## Stage 9: Vegetation and Biodiversity
Climate, altitude, rainfall, and soils shaped forests, grasslands, mangroves, and biodiversity zones.

## Stage 10: Human Settlement
People settled in fertile plains, river valleys, coasts, plateaus, and resource-rich regions.

## Stage 11: Agricultural Regions
Crops developed according to rainfall, soil, temperature, irrigation, and market conditions.

## Stage 12: Industrial Regions
Industries grew near minerals, power, ports, labour, markets, and transport routes.

## Stage 13: Urbanisation
Cities expanded due to trade, industry, administration, services, and migration.

## Stage 14: Environmental Stress
Population growth, urbanisation, pollution, deforestation, and resource use created ecological challenges.

## Stage 15: Disaster and Climate Risk
Earthquakes, floods, cyclones, droughts, landslides, heat waves, and climate change became central geography concerns.

# 44. COMMONLY CONFUSED TERMS

## Science vs Technology
Science explains natural principles. Technology applies knowledge for practical use.

## Discovery vs Invention
Discovery finds what already exists. Invention creates something new.

## Innovation vs Invention
Invention creates a new idea or device. Innovation makes it useful and scalable.

## Data vs Information
Data are raw facts. Information is processed meaningful data.

## AI vs Machine Learning
AI is broader machine intelligence. Machine learning is a method within AI.

## Machine Learning vs Deep Learning
Deep learning uses multi-layer neural networks and is a subset of machine learning.

## Internet vs World Wide Web
Internet is global network. Web is system of pages accessed through internet.

## Virus vs Bacteria
Virus needs host cell to replicate. Bacteria are living single-celled organisms.

## Antibiotic vs Antiviral
Antibiotic acts against bacteria. Antiviral acts against viruses.

## Vaccine vs Medicine
Vaccine prevents disease by training immunity. Medicine treats disease.

## Epidemic vs Pandemic
Epidemic affects a region. Pandemic spreads across countries or continents.

## DNA vs RNA
DNA stores genetic information. RNA helps express and regulate genetic information.

## Gene vs Genome
Gene is a DNA segment. Genome is complete genetic material.

## Biotechnology vs Bioengineering
Biotechnology uses living systems. Bioengineering applies engineering to biological systems.

## Gene Therapy vs Gene Editing
Gene therapy treats disease by genetic material. Gene editing precisely modifies DNA.

## Cloning vs Genetic Engineering
Cloning makes genetic copies. Genetic engineering changes genetic material.

## Nuclear Fission vs Fusion
Fission splits heavy nuclei. Fusion joins light nuclei.

## Radiation vs Radioactivity
Radiation is emitted energy or particles. Radioactivity is spontaneous nuclear decay.

## Satellite vs Launch Vehicle
Satellite is payload placed in orbit. Launch vehicle carries it to space.

## Geostationary vs Geosynchronous
Geostationary appears fixed over equator. Geosynchronous has same orbital period as Earth’s rotation.

## Remote Sensing vs GIS
Remote sensing collects spatial data. GIS stores, analyses, and displays spatial data.

## GPS vs NavIC
GPS is US global navigation system. NavIC is India’s regional navigation system.

## Drone vs UAV
Drone is common term. UAV is technical term for unmanned aerial vehicle.

## Nanotechnology vs Microtechnology
Nanotechnology works at nanometre scale. Microtechnology works at micrometre scale.

## Semiconductor vs Conductor
Semiconductor conductivity can be controlled. Conductor easily allows current.

## Cybersecurity vs Information Security
Cybersecurity protects digital systems. Information security protects information in all forms.

## Encryption vs Hashing
Encryption is reversible with key. Hashing is one-way transformation.

## Authentication vs Authorisation
Authentication verifies identity. Authorisation grants access rights.

# 45. CHRONOLOGICAL STORY TIMELINE OF SCIENCE AND TECHNOLOGY IN INDIA

## Stage 1: Ancient Scientific Traditions
India developed knowledge in mathematics, astronomy, medicine, metallurgy, town planning, water management, and agriculture.

## Stage 2: Harappan Technology
The Indus Civilization showed urban planning, drainage, weights, metallurgy, bead-making, dockyard-like structures, and standardised bricks.

## Stage 3: Vedic and Later Ancient Knowledge
Vedic altar geometry, astronomy for calendars, Ayurveda, grammar, and philosophical reasoning developed.

## Stage 4: Classical Scientific Age
Mathematics, astronomy, medicine, surgery, metallurgy, and linguistics advanced through scholars such as Aryabhata, Sushruta, Charaka, Panini, and Varahamihira.

## Stage 5: Medieval Knowledge Systems
Astronomy, metallurgy, irrigation, textiles, architecture, medicine, and craft technologies continued with regional innovation.

## Stage 6: Colonial Science and Institutions
Modern scientific institutions, surveys, railways, telegraph, universities, and laboratories emerged under colonial conditions.

## Stage 7: Early Post-Independence Scientific Base
India built institutions in atomic energy, space, agriculture, medicine, engineering, and industrial research.

## Stage 8: Green Revolution and Agricultural Science
HYV seeds, irrigation, fertilisers, pesticides, and agricultural research improved foodgrain production.

## Stage 9: Space and Nuclear Capability
India developed indigenous launch vehicles, satellites, nuclear reactors, and strategic technologies.

## Stage 10: Information Technology Rise
Software, telecom, digital services, and IT exports transformed India’s economy and governance.

## Stage 11: Biotechnology and Health Technology
Vaccines, diagnostics, genomics, biopharma, and biotechnology became major S&T areas.

## Stage 12: Digital Public Infrastructure
Aadhaar, UPI, DigiLocker, DBT, and digital platforms transformed service delivery.

## Stage 13: Emerging Technology Era
AI, quantum, semiconductors, drones, robotics, nanotech, green hydrogen, and cyber technologies became strategic priorities.

## Stage 14: Sustainability and Resilience
Science and technology increasingly focus on climate action, disaster management, clean energy, water, health security, and inclusive development.
